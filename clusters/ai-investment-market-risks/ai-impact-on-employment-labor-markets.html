Description

This subcluster examines AI's direct effects on employment patterns, job displacement, and labor market dynamics. Articles analyze workforce data from Census Bureau surveys, job posting platforms like Adzuna, and employment statistics to track AI's impact on hiring, particularly for entry-level positions and young workers aged 22-25. Key themes include sectoral employment shifts, occupational exposure to AI technologies, and changing demand for different skill levels. Unlike sibling subclusters focused on capital investment flows or AI performance metrics, this group emphasizes human workforce consequences, using labor economics research and demographic analysis to document employment "churn," job market polarization, and automation's evolving role in replacing versus augmenting workers across industries.
