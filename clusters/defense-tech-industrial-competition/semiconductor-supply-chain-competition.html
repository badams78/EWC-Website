Description

This subcluster examines the strategic competition between the US and China over semiconductor supply chains and manufacturing capabilities. Articles focus on chip export controls, particularly around Nvidia's advanced AI chips (H200, Blackwell), Chinese firms like SMIC and DeepSeek navigating restrictions, and domestic manufacturing investments by both nations. Coverage draws from trade publications, government policy announcements, and industry analysis, with perspectives spanning geopolitical strategy and technical capabilities. The subcluster emphasizes the intersection of national security policy and commercial competition in critical technologies, distinguishing itself through its focus on supply chain dynamics rather than broader defense technology development or military applications.
