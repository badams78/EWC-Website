Description

This subcluster focuses on military alliance formation and strategic cooperation between the US, Japan, and Taiwan in response to China's growing military presence in the Indo-Pacific. Articles primarily cover defense spending increases, military base expansions, joint command structures, and arms deals, particularly Japan's shift from pacifist policies to active military buildup and Taiwan's defensive acquisitions. Content draws from military leadership statements, defense budget announcements, and geopolitical analyses from major financial and defense publications. Unlike sibling clusters examining supply chain vulnerabilities or broader defense spending patterns, this subcluster specifically addresses bilateral and trilateral security partnerships and their operational military dimensions in the Pacific theater.
