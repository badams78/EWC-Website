Description

This subcluster examines global competition for critical minerals essential to defense and technology manufacturing, with particular focus on China's market dominance and Western efforts to reduce supply chain dependencies. Articles analyze rare earth elements, lithium, copper, and other strategic materials through trade policy, mining investment, and stockpiling initiatives. Content draws from industry reports, government policy announcements, and geopolitical analysis. Unlike sibling clusters focused on finished semiconductors or military spending, this subcluster addresses upstream raw material control and extraction as foundational elements of technological sovereignty and national security strategy.
