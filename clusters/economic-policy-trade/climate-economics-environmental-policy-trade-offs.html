Description

This subcluster examines the economic trade-offs and policy tensions inherent in environmental and climate initiatives. Articles analyze the costs of decarbonization, energy transition economics, and geopolitical implications of green policies. Common perspectives include economic forecasting of climate policy impacts, efficiency versus environmental protection debates, and international competitiveness concerns. Data sources span carbon intensity metrics, trade flows, and GDP projections. Unlike its sibling focusing on pure economic performance comparisons, this subcluster specifically addresses how environmental policies reshape economic structures, trade relationships, and global power dynamics through the lens of climate economics.
