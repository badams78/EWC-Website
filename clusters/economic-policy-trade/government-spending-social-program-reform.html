Description

This subcluster examines government fiscal policy through the lens of social program sustainability and spending reform. Articles analyze healthcare programs (ACA, Medicaid, Medicare), Social Security, and broader federal expenditure patterns using data from CBO, KFF, and academic research. Content focuses on trust fund solvency, benefit eligibility changes, and spending growth trajectories. Unlike sibling subclusters that emphasize economic performance metrics or climate-related policies, this group specifically addresses entitlement program challenges and reform proposals. Coverage includes both domestic U.S. policy analysis and international comparisons, particularly European welfare state reforms, providing empirical evidence on program costs, coverage impacts, and demographic pressures driving policy changes.
