Description

This subcluster examines the structural inefficiencies driving America's exceptionally high infrastructure construction costs compared to international peers. Articles analyze specific cost breakdowns, regulatory bottlenecks, labor market dynamics, and procurement policies that inflate project expenses across transit, highways, and public works. Common sources include academic research, government surveys of state transportation departments, and comparative international cost analyses. Unlike sibling subclusters focused on broader economic performance or fiscal policy debates, this collection specifically dissects the operational and institutional factors behind construction cost overruns, drawing on engineering economics and public administration research to explain why American infrastructure projects consistently exceed global benchmarks.
