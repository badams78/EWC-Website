Description

This subcluster examines the intersection of tax policy and healthcare economics, focusing on how fiscal policies affect healthcare costs, pharmaceutical pricing, and health system financing. Articles analyze corporate tax strategies in pharmaceuticals, Medicare funding sustainability, and international comparisons of health spending efficiency. Common data sources include Bureau of Economic Analysis statistics, OECD healthcare metrics, and academic research on earnings variability and income inequality's health impacts. Unlike sibling subclusters that address broader economic performance or environmental trade-offs, this group specifically targets healthcare sector taxation, price controls, and the fiscal mechanisms underlying health systems across developed economies.
