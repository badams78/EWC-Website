Description

This subcluster examines declining academic achievement and educational performance trends in the United States through standardized assessment data. Articles primarily analyze results from NAEP, PISA, and OECD surveys, documenting concerning patterns including stagnant test scores since 2000, widening achievement gaps, and increased chronic absenteeism. The focus centers on quantitative performance metrics like reading and math proficiency rates, with particular attention to learning loss during and after the pandemic. Coverage includes partisan variations in educational outcomes and unfavorable international comparisons showing the US lagging behind countries like Germany, Scandinavia, and Japan in adult skill assessments.
