Description

This subcluster examines the quantitative relationship between cognitive abilities and economic returns to education through empirical research. Articles analyze standardized test scores, IQ measurements, and longitudinal studies from sources like British birth cohorts, Norwegian military data, and Armed Forces aptitude tests. The focus is on measuring cognitive skill premiums, educational wage returns, and ability-earnings correlations across populations. Unlike sibling clusters examining performance gaps, admissions policies, or generational mobility, this subcluster specifically quantifies how measurable cognitive abilities translate into labor market outcomes, often finding substantial earnings premiums for higher cognitive skills and educational attainment.
