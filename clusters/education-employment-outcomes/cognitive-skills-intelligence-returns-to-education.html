Description

This subcluster examines the quantitative relationship between cognitive abilities, intelligence measures, and educational returns in labor markets. Articles predominantly utilize longitudinal datasets, twin studies, military aptitude tests, and international comparative data to establish causal links between cognitive skills and earnings premiums. Research focuses on IQ correlations with educational attainment, social skills returns, and cross-national cognitive performance variations. Unlike sibling subclusters addressing demographic gaps or institutional factors, this collection emphasizes psychometric measurement and individual-level cognitive determinants of economic success, often drawing from behavioral economics and psychology literature to quantify talent constraints and skill premiums.
