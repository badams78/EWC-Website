Description

This subcluster examines affirmative action policies and racial considerations in educational admissions, spanning from kindergarten gifted programs to elite universities and military academies. Articles analyze demographic enrollment patterns, test score distributions across racial groups, and public opinion on race-conscious admissions policies. Data sources include institutional enrollment statistics, standardized test results, polling data from organizations like Pew Research, and legal challenges from groups like Students for Fair Admissions. Unlike sibling clusters focusing on post-graduation outcomes or general achievement metrics, this subcluster specifically addresses the contentious intersection of race, merit, and access in educational gatekeeping mechanisms.
