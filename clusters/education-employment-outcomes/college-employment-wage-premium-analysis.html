Description

This subcluster examines the evolving relationship between higher education credentials and labor market outcomes, focusing on employment rates, wage premiums, and job placement quality for college graduates. Articles analyze unemployment statistics, enrollment trends, and the diminishing value of college degrees in securing appropriate employment. Data sources include labor statistics, enrollment figures, and wage surveys tracking graduates aged 20-24. Unlike sibling clusters examining academic performance metrics or generational wealth patterns, this subcluster specifically quantifies the economic return on college investment and documents the convergence of employment outcomes between college and non-college workers, revealing challenges to traditional higher education value propositions.
