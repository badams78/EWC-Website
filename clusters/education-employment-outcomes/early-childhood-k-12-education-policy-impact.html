Description

This subcluster examines the effectiveness and impacts of early childhood through K-12 educational policies and interventions. It encompasses research on universal pre-K programs, charter schools, competitive academic programs, and specialized educational approaches, drawing from longitudinal studies, meta-analyses, and large-scale policy evaluations. Articles frequently analyze fade-out effects, differential outcomes across demographic groups, and cost-benefit assessments of educational investments. Unlike sibling clusters focused on performance metrics or demographic gaps, this subcluster specifically evaluates policy mechanisms and institutional reforms within primary and secondary education systems, emphasizing implementation outcomes rather than broader labor market or socioeconomic trends.
