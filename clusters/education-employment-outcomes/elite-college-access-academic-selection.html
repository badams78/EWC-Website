Description

This subcluster examines the relationship between elite college attendance, standardized testing, and economic outcomes. Articles analyze admissions policies at highly selective institutions, particularly focusing on SAT/ACT score requirements and their predictive value for academic success. Common data sources include longitudinal studies of Ivy-Plus colleges, public university admissions records, and standardized test performance by demographic groups. The research emphasizes how returns to elite education vary by student characteristics, with particular attention to differential outcomes for underrepresented minorities and students from less-educated families. Unlike sibling clusters that broadly examine educational attainment or general labor market outcomes, this subcluster specifically targets the premium associated with attending the most selective institutions and the role of academic merit measures in both admissions and subsequent career success.
