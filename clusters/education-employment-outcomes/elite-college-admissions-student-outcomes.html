Description

This subcluster examines the relationship between elite college admissions practices and subsequent student outcomes, focusing particularly on standardized testing requirements and selectivity effects. Articles analyze longitudinal data from Ivy-Plus institutions and selective universities to measure returns to college prestige after controlling for student quality metrics like SAT/ACT scores. Common themes include differential outcomes by race and socioeconomic background, with findings that elite college premiums persist for underrepresented minorities and first-generation students even after ability controls. Unlike sibling clusters examining broader educational trends or general employment outcomes, this subcluster specifically targets admissions policies at top-tier institutions and their causal effects on earnings and career trajectories, often using regression discontinuity designs around test score thresholds.
