Description

This subcluster examines the intersection of educational attainment and relationship formation patterns, with particular focus on how gender disparities in college completion affect marriage markets and household formation. Articles analyze longitudinal demographic data spanning decades, customer account information from major financial institutions, and cross-national research to explore themes like assortative mating by education level, declining marriage rates among non-college populations, and evolving gender pay gaps. Unlike sibling subclusters that focus purely on academic performance or workplace dynamics, this group uniquely connects educational outcomes to intimate relationship patterns and family structure changes, incorporating both economic and sociological perspectives on how educational gender reversals reshape modern partnership formation.
