Description

This subcluster examines how economic advantages and disadvantages transfer across generations, focusing on intergenerational income mobility, wealth persistence, and factors that enable or constrain economic opportunity. Articles utilize longitudinal datasets spanning multiple generations, survey data like GSS and PSID, and demographic analyses to track families over time. Research encompasses geographic mobility effects, educational transmission mechanisms, and policy interventions that could break cycles of disadvantage. Unlike sibling clusters that focus on immediate educational or labor market outcomes, this subcluster takes a multigenerational view, examining how parental income, family structure, and regional factors shape children's long-term economic prospects and the persistence of economic inequality across family lineages.
