Description

This subcluster examines how economic status transmits across generations, focusing on income mobility patterns and factors influencing economic progression over time. Articles utilize longitudinal datasets like PSID, multi-generational family data, and survey research to analyze whether children exceed their parents' income levels and what drives these outcomes. Research explores geographic mobility effects, educational attainment impacts, family structure influences, and wealth persistence among ultra-high earners. Unlike sibling clusters that focus on immediate educational performance or current labor market dynamics, this subcluster takes a longitudinal view spanning decades and generations to understand economic mobility trajectories and intergenerational economic relationships.
