Description

This subcluster examines how technological change and economic restructuring have created divergent labor market outcomes based on educational attainment. Articles analyze declining employment rates and social status among non-college workers, particularly young men, using longitudinal data from 1980-2024 spanning multiple countries. Research draws from employment surveys, housing data, and social capital measurements to document geographic and demographic disparities in job quality, wage premiums, and workforce participation. Unlike sibling clusters focusing on academic performance metrics or managerial effectiveness, this subcluster emphasizes structural economic shifts, social isolation, and the erosion of traditional career pathways for workers without higher education credentials.
