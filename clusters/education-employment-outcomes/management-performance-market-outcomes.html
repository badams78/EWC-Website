Description

This subcluster examines the relationship between management quality, employee performance, and market outcomes through empirical research and case studies. Articles typically draw from corporate surveys, stock market data, CEO selection studies, and longitudinal employment records to analyze how managerial effectiveness translates into measurable business results. The research spans historical market volatility analysis, compensation strategies, and performance attribution studies from sources like NBER, Census Bureau surveys, and financial firms. Unlike its sibling subcluster focused on academic learning metrics and educational achievement gaps, this subcluster concentrates on workplace performance dynamics and their broader economic implications through market mechanisms and corporate governance structures.
