Description

This subcluster examines performance measurement and management effectiveness across corporate, financial, and labor market contexts. Articles analyze manager quality through experimental assignments, CEO selection processes, and compensation structures, while exploring employee performance patterns using statistical comparisons and behavioral studies. Data sources include US Census Bureau surveys, NBER research, stock market analysis from 1929-2022, and corporate case studies. Unlike its sibling subcluster focused on academic learning outcomes, this collection emphasizes workplace productivity, management practices, and market performance metrics, drawing connections between individual performance trends and broader economic patterns through empirical research and quantitative analysis.
