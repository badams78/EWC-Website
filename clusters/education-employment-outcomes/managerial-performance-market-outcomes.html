Description

This subcluster examines the relationship between managerial effectiveness and broader economic performance through empirical analysis of corporate and market data. Articles typically draw from large-scale datasets including US Census Bureau management surveys, S&P 500 earnings data, executive search studies, and longitudinal firm performance metrics spanning decades. The focus centers on quantifying management quality's impact on stock market volatility, firm valuations, and macroeconomic cycles, often using experimental or quasi-experimental methodologies. Unlike its sibling subcluster on Academic Achievement & Learning Assessment, which emphasizes educational institutions and student outcomes, this area concentrates on corporate leadership effectiveness and its transmission to market-level phenomena and economic stability.
