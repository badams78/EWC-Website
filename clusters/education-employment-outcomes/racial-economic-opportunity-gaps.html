Description

This subcluster examines racial disparities in economic mobility and employment outcomes through empirical research and policy analysis. Articles predominantly feature Raj Chetty's influential mobility research alongside studies using tax records, W-2 data, and labor statistics from 1998-2024. The focus centers on intergenerational income gaps between racial groups, with particular attention to black-white opportunity differences and immigration effects. Unlike sibling clusters examining general academic performance or wage premiums, this subcluster specifically investigates how race intersects with economic advancement, featuring prominent economists' work on structural barriers, unemployment trends, and skill gaps that perpetuate inequality across generations.
