Description

This subcluster examines economic disparities across racial and ethnic groups, focusing on employment rates, wage gaps, and intergenerational mobility differences. Articles predominantly analyze empirical research using tax data, labor statistics, and longitudinal studies, particularly citing work by Raj Chetty and other economists. Coverage spans manufacturing job losses affecting Black workers, immigrant earnings patterns, interracial marriage trends, and unemployment rate variations by race. Unlike sibling subclusters that focus on educational achievement metrics or specific demographic segments, this cluster emphasizes systemic economic inequalities and their underlying causes, incorporating both quantitative analyses and policy debates about discrimination versus skill gaps.
