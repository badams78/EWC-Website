Description

This subcluster examines methodological issues and credibility challenges within academic research that informs education and employment policy. Articles focus on replication crises, research misconduct, publication bias, and factors that compromise scientific integrity. Common themes include retraction rates, failed replications, statistical manipulation, and the politicization of research findings. Data sources include meta-analyses of academic publications, citation studies, and grant funding research. Unlike sibling subclusters that analyze specific educational or employment outcomes, this group critiques the research infrastructure itself, questioning the reliability of studies that underpin policy decisions and highlighting systemic problems in peer review and academic incentives.
