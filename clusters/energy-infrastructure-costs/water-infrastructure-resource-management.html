Description

This subcluster examines water infrastructure development, resource allocation, and scarcity challenges across regional and global scales. Articles analyze government water policies, interstate allocation agreements, groundwater depletion impacts on agriculture, and climate-driven supply stress. Data sources include Federal Reserve analyses, regional water authority reports, and agricultural impact studies. Coverage spans major infrastructure projects like China's Yarlung Tsangpo dam alongside localized pricing and allocation issues in the US Southwest. Unlike sibling subclusters focused on energy production or technological breakthroughs, this examines water as both a critical resource and infrastructure challenge requiring policy intervention and economic trade-offs.
