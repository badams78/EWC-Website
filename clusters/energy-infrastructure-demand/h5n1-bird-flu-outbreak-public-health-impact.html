Description

This subcluster focuses on the ongoing H5N1 avian influenza outbreak and its expanding public health implications. Articles predominantly track disease surveillance data from CDC and WHO, documenting viral spread across species barriers from wild birds to dairy cattle, pigs, and humans. Coverage emphasizes epidemiological analysis, genomic sequencing results, and mortality statistics, with particular attention to new viral variants like D1.1 and transmission patterns among agricultural workers. Unlike sibling subclusters examining energy production capacity or technological innovation, this collection centers on infectious disease monitoring and outbreak response, connecting to energy infrastructure through potential impacts on agricultural supply chains and economic stability during health emergencies.
