Description

This subcluster examines the economic impacts of minimum wage policies and government income support programs on workers and households. Articles analyze data from sources like CBO, CPS, HUD, and randomized controlled trials to assess how wage mandates, public housing assistance, tax credits, and direct cash transfers affect employment levels, household income, and poverty rates. The research spans both theoretical policy analysis and empirical studies, including natural experiments and controlled interventions. Unlike sibling subclusters focused on broader wage trends or aggregate employment patterns, this collection specifically investigates targeted government interventions designed to support low-income workers and families, evaluating both intended benefits and unintended consequences like potential job losses from wage increases.
