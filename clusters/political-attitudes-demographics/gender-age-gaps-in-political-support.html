Description

This subcluster examines demographic divides in political preferences, particularly focusing on generational and gender-based differences in electoral support and policy attitudes. Articles analyze polling data, survey research, and electoral outcomes to understand how age and gender shape political behavior across different countries and contexts. Common sources include major polling organizations like Pew, WSJ-NORC, and academic studies tracking demographic trends over time. The research reveals surprising patterns, such as young men's increasing conservative leanings and persistent gender gaps in religiosity and political trust. Unlike its sibling subcluster on voter demographics and electoral shifts, which focuses on broad population movements and voting patterns, this group specifically isolates age and gender as key variables driving political attitudes and party support across diverse political systems.
