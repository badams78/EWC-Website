Description

This subcluster examines evolving political attitudes across gender and generational lines, particularly focusing on emerging polarization patterns among young voters. Articles analyze survey data from sources like Pew Research, WSJ-NORC polls, and cross-national studies revealing significant gender gaps in political preferences, with particular attention to religious Americans and changing conservative-liberal alignments. The research encompasses both American and European contexts, documenting how traditional demographic voting patterns are shifting. Unlike its sibling subcluster on Voter Demographics & Electoral Shifts, which focuses on broader electoral outcomes and demographic movement, this subcluster specifically isolates the intersection of age and gender as key variables driving contemporary political polarization and attitude formation.
