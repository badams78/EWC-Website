Description

This subcluster examines the evolution and economic drivers of drug epidemics, particularly the opioid crisis and overdose mortality patterns across US regions. Articles analyze CDC mortality data, SSDI disability trends, and international drug market dynamics to understand how economic factors like prescription pricing, black market competition, and regional economic decline influence addiction rates. Coverage spans from fentanyl's displacement of traditional opioids to recent declines in overdose deaths. Unlike sibling clusters focused on housing costs or urban crime, this subcluster specifically tracks drug-related mortality as both a public health crisis and economic phenomenon shaped by pharmaceutical markets and regional socioeconomic conditions.
