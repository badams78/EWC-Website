Description

This subcluster examines drug overdose mortality patterns and opioid crisis dynamics through quantitative analysis of health data trends. Articles predominantly analyze CDC mortality statistics, regional epidemiological patterns, and economic factors driving substance abuse, including prescription pricing and black market drug supply chains. Coverage spans demographic-specific mortality rates, geographic clustering of overdose deaths, and emerging trends in fentanyl versus traditional opioids. Unlike sibling subclusters focused on housing markets or general demographic shifts, this collection specifically targets public health crises as urban policy challenges, emphasizing data-driven approaches to understanding addiction's socioeconomic determinants and regional variations.
