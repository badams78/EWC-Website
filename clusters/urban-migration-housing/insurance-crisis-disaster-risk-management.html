Description

This subcluster examines the growing crisis in property insurance markets driven by escalating climate-related disasters and their cascading effects on housing markets. Articles analyze insurer financial losses, regulatory responses, and risk assessment methodologies, drawing heavily on state regulatory data, reinsurance reports, and catastrophe bond performance metrics. Coverage spans premium increases, insurer insolvencies, policy cancellations, and innovative policy solutions like Florida's property tax reforms. Unlike sibling clusters focusing on broader housing affordability trends or demographic shifts, this subcluster specifically addresses how disaster risk is reshaping insurance markets and creating location-specific housing vulnerabilities that threaten traditional homeownership models.
