Description

This subcluster examines the intersection of natural disasters and insurance market disruptions affecting housing markets, particularly through regulatory analysis and financial data. Articles predominantly feature insurance industry statistics, regulatory filings, catastrophe bond performance metrics, and state-level policy responses to climate-related risks. Coverage emphasizes quantitative analysis from industry sources like Swiss Re, Demotech ratings, and state insurance commissioners. Unlike sibling subclusters focused on broader affordability trends or demographic migration patterns, this collection specifically targets how climate risk and insurance availability create localized housing market distortions, with Florida serving as a primary case study for insurance-driven real estate volatility.
