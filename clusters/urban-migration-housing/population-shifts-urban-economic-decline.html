Description

This subcluster examines demographic and economic shifts driving urban population changes, focusing on out-migration patterns from major metropolitan areas like NYC and California. Articles analyze census data, migration statistics, and employment trends to understand how domestic relocation affects urban economies. Coverage includes the fiscal implications of population loss, sectoral job shifts, and declining mobility rates over decades. Unlike its sibling subcluster on housing affordability which emphasizes market pricing dynamics, this group concentrates on broader demographic flows and their economic consequences for cities experiencing decline or growth transitions.
