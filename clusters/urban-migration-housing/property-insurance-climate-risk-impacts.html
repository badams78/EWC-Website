Description

This subcluster examines the intersection of property insurance markets and climate-driven risks, focusing on how natural disasters and extreme weather events are reshaping housing costs and availability. Articles analyze insurer financial performance, regulatory responses, and market disruptions across states, with particular attention to high-risk regions like Florida. Common sources include industry data from reinsurers, state regulatory filings, and financial analyses of insurance company losses and premium adjustments. Unlike sibling subclusters that focus on broader housing affordability trends or demographic migration patterns, this subcluster specifically targets the insurance sector's role as a critical intermediary between climate risk and housing market stability, documenting how rising catastrophe exposure forces insurers to withdraw from markets or dramatically increase premiums.
