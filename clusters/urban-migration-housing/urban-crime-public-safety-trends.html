Description

This subcluster examines urban crime patterns, policing strategies, and public safety deterioration in American cities. Articles analyze violent crime surges, particularly the 61% increase between 2019-2024, alongside rising youth criminality and public disorder issues like fare evasion and homelessness. Data sources include FBI crime statistics, victimization surveys, and prison population reports. Coverage spans traditional metrics like murder rates and property crime to broader quality-of-life concerns including graffiti and public space misuse. Unlike sibling clusters focused on housing economics or population demographics, this subcluster specifically addresses criminal justice outcomes and urban safety governance challenges.
