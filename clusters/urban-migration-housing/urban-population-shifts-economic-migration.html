Description

This subcluster examines population movements between US cities and their economic drivers, focusing on domestic migration patterns, workforce mobility, and regional economic shifts. Articles analyze census data, employment statistics, and demographic trends to understand why people relocate, particularly the exodus from expensive cities like NYC and California's tech sector decline. The content emphasizes macro-level migration flows, policy impacts on population movement, and economic consequences of demographic shifts. Unlike its sibling focusing on housing affordability and market dynamics, this subcluster prioritizes population mobility patterns and their broader economic implications rather than real estate pricing mechanisms.
