Description

This subcluster examines behavioral patterns, mental health trends, and social outcomes among young people, primarily focusing on American youth aged 12-25. Articles frequently cite government surveys like BLS's American Time Use Survey and CDC data to track changes in social engagement, mental health indicators, and lifestyle behaviors over time. The research emphasizes longitudinal analysis, comparing current youth behaviors to historical patterns, particularly noting declines in social activities like party attendance and shifts in conscientiousness. Unlike sibling subclusters focused on housing economics or demographic migration, this collection prioritizes psychological and social wellness metrics, drawing from academic studies and policy research to understand how contemporary youth experience differs from previous generations in terms of social connection and mental health outcomes.
