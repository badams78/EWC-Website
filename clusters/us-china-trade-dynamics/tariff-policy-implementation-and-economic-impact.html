Description

This subcluster examines the concrete implementation of tariff policies and their measurable economic consequences within US-China trade relations. Articles predominantly feature quantitative analyses from government agencies like the CBO, academic economists, and policy researchers, utilizing product-level pricing data, household impact studies, and revenue forecasting models. The focus centers on pass-through rates, inflation effects, GDP impacts, and distributional consequences across income levels. Unlike its sibling cluster examining broader dollar strength and trade balance dynamics, this subcluster emphasizes granular policy mechanics, specific tariff exemptions, and empirical measurement of immediate economic effects on consumers, retailers, and government revenues.
